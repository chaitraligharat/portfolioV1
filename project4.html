Smart Lid

Helping visually impaired cook independently.

My role : Product design, UX Design

Duration : 3 months

Challenge

Develop an idea around the theme 'Glance' and apply teh course concepts learnt in the HCI course on it. This was my first independent project that I executed while learning more about the field of HCI. Learning concepts and executing them while juggling work was certainly a challenge.

Background

While working as a Software developer, I got introduced to the field of UX. I decided to learn more about the field and took up an online course on Coursera taught by Prof. Scott Klemmer.

User Reseach

The first step was Needfinding by talking to users. I talked to people belonging to different age groups around me. Each had their own way of reminding themselves of things they had to do, from sticky notes on their laptops to writing down things in a diary to setting an alarm.

Design inspirations

An interesting next step for the project was to analyse current systems around and draw inspirations. The idea was to draw inspirations from varied sources and draw learnings. I took inpirations from physical object like calendars which served as alternatives to what I was trying to achieve through my system, post-its for their simplicity and friends reminding people of things that need to be done.

Storyboarding

The next step was thinking about how might a user use my application in everyday life. I drew two likely scenarios. This helped me picturise what possible workflows I might want to support with my application.

Low Fidelity Prototypes

Next came the low fidelity prototypes. I sketched these first on paper and then made quick mockkups on Balsamiq. I liked how this tool allowed me to make quick mockups by putting together commonly used elements.

High Fidelity Prototypes

After having a clear idea of the interface and the interactions. I went on to fine tune my UI through high-fidelity prototypes.

Peer review through heuristic testing

My peers on Coursera evaluated my design based on the heuristics provided and came up with suggestions that I could work on before full fleged testing. I think this principle will be tremendously helpful in real life projects as well for getting quick feedback and at the same time it'll save valuable resources. I worked on a low-fi redesign based on the peer evaluation.

User testing

I produced user testing script and put my high fidelity prototype and the redesign though user tests. Having a redisgn in place helped the users really articulate which one was better and explain their concerns with each design.

Lessons Learnt

An overview of the UX process.

Importance of comparative tests of an interface.

Importance of having low-fi prototypes in place before advancing to the high-fi version.
