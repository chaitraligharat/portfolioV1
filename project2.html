Explorify

Encouraging people to spend more time offline.

My role : Product Design, UX Design, UI Design

Duration : 3 months

Challenge

Coin a project idea around the theme of community and design an interactive prototype as final deliverable.

Background

An independent, semester long project for SI 582 - Introduction to Interaction Design developed my applying all the concepts learnt in the class on weekly basis.

Personas

Previous Next

One of the earliest steps in the process was to know who would want the application. After generating about five personas, I zeroed in on these two as my primary and secondary personas. Also generated a non-persona in the process..

Sketching

The after knowing what the users were looking for, it was time to ideate and come up with as many solutions as I could. I came up with sketches of eight different solutions to solve this problem. The sketching technique really helped in brainstorming and putting all my ideas out of my head onto paper.

Brainstorming

After sketching many ideas, I had to converge on a solution to work on and develop. I brainstormed with my peers and analysed each solution. An interesting outcome of this brainstorming session was combining two of the solutions to create my final solution.

Question-Options-Criteria : I used this method to fine tune my selected solution. I liked how this method helped me dig into details of different aspects of my solution and select plan of action.

Previous Next

After QOC I sketched the alternatives for possible screen interactions. This was a preliminary step before paper prototypes.

Paper prototypes

Paper prototypes helped me design interactions and test them with peers and get some feedback before I began work on prototypes of higher fidelity.

High Fidelity Prototypes

After series of iterations, I designed high-fidelity prototypes. Here, I faced a problem of how to represent the events that a person might want to attend on a particular day. Especially showing overlapping events. I wanted to give the user an overview of what their day looks like and at the same time show events clearly. This came out be more complicated than I thought and in the end, I settled for giving the user a rough overview of the day and gave up on showing exact start and end times.

Lessons Learnt

  • Giving and receiving feedback.
  • Iteration is important.
  • How to advance from low-fi to high-fi.
  • Sketching many many ideas for brainstorming.
