Twitter Backgrounds

Season themed Twitter background for Nicols Arboretum.

Tools : Photoshop

Previous Next

Thoughts...

This assignment taught me a lot about blending, colors and creating harmony in images. The whole creation process for these backgrounds was iterative. This was the first time I was dabbling with Photoshop. Hence, getting little things the way I wanted them meant searching online or asking my mentors at School.

The first round of work submitted had highly saturated background colors which interfered with the content on display. The solid color that I had implemented based on the planning phase did not quite work out well. While I was afraid that decreasing the saturation might impact the visibilty of text on the left, I tried it out. Turns out it didn't affect the visibility of the content at all! Important lesson learnt : Try stuff first and see for yourself!

Another important technique this assignment taught me was use of tiles to extend the background. Searching images that worked with the theme itself was a challenge. Once I found images, they were all of different sizes. Stretching an image to cover the entire canvas was not an option because the smaller images ran a risk of getting pixelated. Tiles and gradients came to my rescue here.

I also learnt about how color harmonies work, I decided to go with contrast color harmony as I wanted to experiment a bit not stick to the more obvious tetradic color harmony corresponding to four seasons. I reworked images to fit them into the color scheme - made summer image a bit more blue, reduced the saturation of the fall image which was bright orange and darkened the winter image a bit.
