Littman® TeleSteth™

Simplify workflow and make the application more usable.

My role : UX Design

Duration : 2 hours

Challenge

The two target users of the 3M Littman stethoscope doctors and operators interacting with patients remotely had to go through a series of complicated steps to setup the stethoscope and use it. Our job was to improve this whole experience for the users. We were given about two hours to understand the system, ideate and sketch improvements.

Background

The 3M Littman® TeleSteth™ System allows clinicians to evaluate patient sounds remotely and in real-time through an interactive user interface. Users of the system can initiate and join consulting sessions, pair the software with their award-winning Littman® electronic stethoscopes, and collaborate over live auscultation sounds.

Ideation

After understanding the problem statement, we had a hands-on to understand the product first hand. Then came the ideation phase where we got together and jotted our ideas on paper.

Defining scope

Given the time, narrowing down the problem we were to address was very important. We decided to zero in upon problem areas like pairing the stethoscope and setting up session as our primary focus. Apart from this, we also decided to work out smaller very obvious fixes like removal of disabled tabs, etc.

Sketching

After deciding the focus, we set about sketching our solution. I was one of the people who sketched the solution. We ensured that we had all the screens of the solution on the same page to present our solution and the interactions better.

Solution

We identified that since doctors and operators are part of the same system, their login process should we the same. We also simplified the login UI and new session initiation. We also streamlined the flow and made it intuitive. Pairing a stethoscope now had a well defined tutorial. Video chat to co-ordinate the remote session better was incorporated.

Lessons Learnt

  • How to quickly ideate and come up with quick and dirty solutions.
  • Presenting solutions to an audience.
