Smart Lid

Helping visually impaired cook.

My role : Product Design, UX Design, UI Design

Duration : 2 months

Challenge

Identify a real problem that is faced by people with special needs and solve it by presenting a unique solution.

Background

The theme of CHI - Student Design Competition 2016 was 'Doing Good'. We had to work as a group and come up with a solution that addressed a real problem faced by people who could not use maintstream technology due to physical, cognitive or sensory impairment.

Personas

The first step was knowing the user. This was an important step in giving a concrete definition to our problem statement and then trying to solve it.

Brainstorming

The identification of the problem and solution both required intensive ideation and brainstorming. We were looking for a very specific problem to solve and not come up with a solution first that addressed many problems but wasn't usable. Finally we settled on the problem of the problems faced by visually impaired while cooking.

Sketching

During and after brainstorming, sketching as a tool helped us a lot. The whole time we were trying different solutions and evaluating their feasibility. It also helped clarify workflows.

Prototypes

I worked on prototypes of the phone application part of the solution. My aim was to produce interactions which could be used by partial visual impairment as well as total visual impairment. I introduced high contrast and kept the UI minimalist so that the elements would easily stand out.

Lessons Learnt

  • Narrowing down and zeroing on problem through consensus.
  • Taking accessibility into account while designing applications.
