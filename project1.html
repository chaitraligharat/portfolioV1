Pix Up

Uber for X startup for hauling

My role : Product Design, User Research, UX Design, Usability Testing

Team project for UMSI Design Clinic

Duration : 6 months

Challenge

To find whether the idea would actually work. If yes, develop it into something tangible that can be passed on to developers.

Background

UMSI Design Clinic helps startups realise their ideas. Pix Up was my first project with the Design Clinic.

Pix Up follows 'Uber for X' business model to bring together the communities of users who are looking for quick, easy and affordable options for quick delivery and college students who have the time, ability and vehicle to help and are also willing to make some extra money.

Process

Through a series of client meetings, we understood what exactly the client had in mind. Then we kicked off a three month long journey to turn Pix Up into reality.

User interviews and Brainstorming

We began with user interviews, talking to middle aged people who have stayed in Ann Arbor their whole life who were our target customers and college students who were our drivers. Sherry and I teamed up to conduct the two interviews - one customer and one driver. By the end of our interviews, we reached the conclusion that there was indeed a demand for such a service and the target users would indeed use it.

Our next step was generating user personas. Personas were developed by the whole team through brainstorming and consolidating all the information gathered through user interviews.

Personas

We generated three user personas based on out interviews : Sharon, Dave, Jackson.

We decided to concentrate Dave’s and Sharon’s persona while developing the product as the Jackson persona had very little motivation to sign up for delivery service to earn money. We also thought a mobile app would be the best for the driver persona while a desktop website would be best for customer, considering the age group we were targeting and our user interviews.

Storyboards

Now that we knew our users, the next step was to determine how will our users use the app. These required intensive brainstorming and many questions came up. Will this be a quick same day delivery service? Or will users book their delivery days before the actual delivery? How will the driver discover the orders around them? How to ensure that the customer’s order is picked up?

We decided to go for the quick same day delivery service, following discussions with client and the kind of product they envisioned Pix Up to be. I was an active participant in the brainstorming sessions and helped the team form a coherent idea for the storyboards. One of the team members pointed out Bitstrips as a useful resource for creating storyboards. The final results were two storyboards - one for customer, another for the driver. An interesting outcome of the storyboarding process was that we came to the conclusion that for the kind of service we are offering, a mobile application made most sense, even on the customer end.

Comparative Analysis

We now knew our customers, the next step was to know what our direct and indirect competitors were doing. We zeroed in on Kanga, Dolly, UberRush, Amazon Fresh, Google Express, UPS and UHaul to be included in the analysis. I analysed Kanga and UHaul. The findings were consolidated and a detailed report was produced which touched upon detailed analysis, method and key findings. We also decided that we need separate driver and customer applications based on our findings.

Prototypes

After intensive ideation, we broke up in teams of two with one team in charge of the customer wireframes and the other in charge of the driver wireframe. I was responsible for the customer side wireframe. White board and paper were our initial tools while chalking out the process. Our aim was to help the customer place the order in minimum possible steps and to avoid asking excessive information on a single page. This process helped me sharpen my interaction design skills and got me acquainted to Adobe Illustrator.

After creating the customer wireframes and comparing both customer and driver wireframes, we switched to creating high fidelity prototypes. Color schemes, logos and the final polished UI came through. I worked on the interaction design of the customer side UI.

User Tests

We conducted user tests on the first version of the prototypes generated. I ran two user tests, one for the driver wireframe and one for customer wireframe. Our test results reflected that there were still things that our prototypes lacked. Due to our focus on lesser steps on customer end, we were not making the elements on the UI clear enough to the user. We decided to go back and introduce an additional step so that we explained the elements on the UI better given the limited space we had at our disposal on the mobile screen.

Final Delivery!
